How to File an FIR (First Information Report) in India

A First Information Report (FIR) is a written document prepared by the police when they receive information about the commission of a cognizable offence. Filing an FIR is your legal right, and it sets the criminal justice process in motion.

Steps to File an FIR:

  1. Go to the Police Station:

    Visit the police station that has jurisdiction over the area where the offence occurred. If you are unsure, you can file a "Zero FIR" at any police station, which will then be transferred to the correct jurisdiction.

  2. Inform the Officer in Charge:

    Meet the Station House Officer (SHO) or the officer in charge and provide them with all the details of the offence. Be clear and concise.

  3. Provide Written Information:

    It is advisable to provide the information in writing. Include the following details:

    • Your name and contact details.
    • Date, time, and location of the offence.
    • Details of the offence and the accused (if known).
    • Names and contact details of witnesses (if any).
    • Any other relevant information.
  4. Read the FIR Carefully:

    After the police officer writes the FIR, read it carefully to ensure all the information is accurate. If there are any discrepancies, point them out and request corrections.

  5. Sign the FIR:

    Sign the FIR after you are satisfied with its content. You have the right to receive a free copy of the FIR. Do not leave the police station without getting your copy.

  6. Get the FIR Number:

    Note down the FIR number for future reference. This number is crucial for tracking the progress of your case.

  7. If the Police Refuse to File an FIR:

    If the police refuse to file your FIR, you can take the following steps:

    • Submit a written complaint to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP) of the district.
    • File a complaint under Section 156(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) before a Magistrate.
    • File a complaint with the State Human Rights Commission or the National Human Rights Commission.

Important Points:

  • Filing an FIR is your legal right.
  • Do not delay in filing an FIR, especially in serious offences.
  • Provide accurate and complete information.
  • Keep a copy of the FIR for your records.
